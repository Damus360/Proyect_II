Informatic Engineering

Informatics Engineering is a field that focuses on the design, development, and implementation of computer systems and software. It combines theoretical knowledge with practical skills, covering areas like:

1st year's Subjects General Description Learn more
Fundamentals of Computer Engineering This subject aims to provide a global vision of Computer Engineering in both academic and professional points of view. The course gives the students a grasp of the fundamentals on which this engineering is based (mathematics, physics, anthropology and ethics), the future professional careers and the role of a computer engineer in today's society.
Mathematics for Engineering The subject of Mathematics for Engineering I applies and develops knowledge of linear algebra and optimization in real functions of a variable, offering examples of practical application of the different concepts covered. It wants to be an eminently practical subject that guides the student in solving complex problems, not only in solving exercises, and that adapts as much as possible to the reality of the jobs that will be encountered in professional practice.
Logic and Critical Thinking The subject provides the necessary foundation and key skills for the subsequent development of the capacity for action in the field of Big Data together with technological knowledge.
Introduction to Programming The subject focuses on problem solving, building programs that solve simple problems. To do this, it starts from the design of the algorithm, using modular structures and different programming methods, and reaches both its implementation in a structured programming language, using fundamental coding control structures and basic data types, and its subsequent compilation, debugging and documentation.
Knowledge and Skills Management of the Person It is a peculiar subject, which exists to help you in your personal growth during the university stage.
Discrete Mathematics The subject deals with various areas of mathematics such as set theory, relations, finite and modular arithmetic and graphs, among others, of interest as foundations in the training of the future engineer in relation to the processing and storage of information. in computers, to the design and development of algorithms, to the organization inherent to databases based on relationships between sets and to the basis of data structures and operating systems.
Introduction to Cybersecurity The objective of this subject is to teach the basic concepts around Cybersecurity in both the business and consumer world.
Operating Systems Administration The subject Administration of Operating Systems I introduces the student to the role of the operating system within a computer by studying its main functions, presenting the basic file management techniques and training the student for the installation, basic configuration and management of the operating systems.
Mathematics Engineering II The course aims to provide students with sufficient mathematical skills to address common problems in computer engineering, recognizing their interdisciplinary nature and following a structured process of orientation, representation, decision-making, and action to find solutions.
Electronics and Computer Technology The course aims to teach the fundamentals of electronics, focusing on their role in the physical operation of computers, peripherals, and communication systems, while introducing the principles of logical design for creating simple digital circuits.
The Company and its Processes This course introduces students to the business environment with a focus on the role of ICT in organizations, helping them develop critical thinking and a deeper understanding of business processes. It covers the evolution of businesses, turning ideas into viable projects, basic accounting and finance, ICT tools and systems, and managing key business processes to achieve organizational goals.
Object Oriented Programming The course focuses on mastering the analysis, design, implementation, and testing of software using Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) principles. Emphasis is placed on practical application, particularly during the implementation phase, with C# as the primary programming language to build robust and maintainable applications.
History of Science and Technology The course aims to provide students with a broad understanding of key historical events shaping their culture, emphasizing the impact of scientific development on modern history. It seeks to contextualize their profession within its historical evolution, highlighting its connections to philosophy, mathematics, and physics, and its progression through advancements in technology, machine manufacturing, and system construction.
2nd year's Subject General Description Learn more
Data Structures and Algorithms This course aims to provide students with both theoretical and practical knowledge, along with the skills to master algorithmic techniques for developing efficient applications while fostering critical thinking. It also emphasizes humanistic aspects, focusing on: anthropological objectives, such as encouraging innovation, truth-seeking, social engagement, and a vocation for service.
Computer Architecture and Organisation This course aims to provide an understanding of computer hardware, including its components, performance criteria, and principles of low-level application development. Students will learn to analyze and evaluate system performance and select appropriate systems for specific needs. Key objectives include assembly language programming, analyzing Von Neumann architecture, optimizing memory access with cache techniques, and enhancing CPU performance using pipelines and parallelism.
Analysis and Design of Information Systems This course focuses on teaching students the methodologies and tools required for designing information systems, emphasizing the analysis of information flow, proper data storage, system states, and internal design. Students will learn to identify and structure the necessary information for system operation using modeling languages, analyze and represent system behavior with modeling tools, define use cases by exploring various scenarios, and map out the information flows required during the conception and lifecycle of a system or software product.
Web Development The primary goal of this course is to equip students with the knowledge needed to develop front-end web applications using proper structures, market-relevant designs, and device adaptability. Students will learn to create website structures in HTML5 that meet modern design and accessibility standards, apply CSS to develop visually appealing and responsive layouts, and utilize the Bootstrap framework for front-end development. Additionally, the course introduces JavaScript for adding interactivity, the JQuery library to simplify JavaScript usage, and explores search engine optimization (SEO) criteria to enhance website visibility.
Projects I This course equips students with the skills to work in teams to solve complex problems by identifying social issues that can be addressed with computer solutions. Students will research these problems, define projects, design and build software applications to meet requirements, and document and professionally present their results. The course emphasizes teamwork in engineering projects and encourages reflection on the societal implications and impact of their work.
Statistics The Statistics course aims to teach students descriptive techniques and data analysis to better understand the world. It covers descriptive statistics, probability calculations, and statistical distributions. Students will learn to estimate population parameters (e.g., mean, variance) for sample comparisons, perform hypothesis testing, and conduct linear regression analysis.
Databases This course on Database Management Systems (DBMS) is a fundamental component of Computer Engineering, aiming to provide students with essential knowledge in database design, query languages, updates, implementation, and administration.
Operating Systems The main goal of this course is to introduce the nature and features of modern operating systems, focusing on their components, structure, and operational mechanisms. Specific objectives include understanding the general structure of contemporary operating systems, learning concurrent programming (POSIX), understanding virtual memory management, and studying the interaction between system hardware and the operating system.
Software Engineering I This course aims to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of software development by balancing the integration of people, material resources, and intellectual resources to create projects that meet client needs, whether explicitly stated or implied, while adhering to constraints of time, quality, and service. While traditionally focused on teaching techniques and tools to develop functional and client-satisfying software, this approach has often fallen short by either neglecting the human element or prioritizing economic objectives over quality work.
Anthropology and Social Responsibility This course aims to help students explore their identity and humanity by addressing questions like “Who am I?” and “What is the human being?” It encourages them to recognize the ontological dignity of all people, live consistently with this awareness, and become transformative agents in the world.
3rd year's Subject General Description Learn more
Artificial Intelligence I This course aims to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the fundamental principles of Artificial Intelligence (AI), exploring its dual role as a science replicating human cognition and as an engineering discipline developing intelligent systems to complement human activities.
Software Engineering II Software Engineering II aims to deepen students understanding of the complexities of software system development by focusing on organizing material and human resources for sustainable and high-quality software. The course emphasizes defining and managing software products based on critical quality attributes, understanding and applying agile methodologies like Scrum and DevOps, and transforming requirements into quantifiable work structures. It highlights the evolutionary nature of software and the importance of maintainability, managing product quality through metrics and SMART objectives, and understanding various software testing approaches.
Software Development and Integration The main objective of the course is to study the different strategies for software integration in the field of Service Oriented Architecture, understood as a software architecture for the development of applications that, based on components in charge of offering a certain service, implementing business processes.
Human Computer Interaction This course focuses on applying methodological knowledge to the life cycle of graphical user interfaces, emphasizing human factors and accessibility to create user-centered designs. Students will learn to understand psychological factors in human-computer interaction, develop user-centered designs through real-life case studies, utilize interface modeling tools, and apply techniques to reduce subjectivity in user-centered system modeling.
Computational Complexity Key topics include the theoretical basis and historical challenges of computational complexity, such as the “P=NP?” problem, designing algorithms with minimal computational cost, and applying optimization techniques like Divide and Conquer, recursive algorithms, greedy algorithms, dynamic programming, and backtracking.
Systems Administration The course addresses integration and communication challenges between operating systems and their role as platforms for vendor-provided and custom software solutions. Students will gain the skills to select and install appropriate systems based on service requirements and environment constraints, while also exploring virtualization infrastructure and service models to make informed decisions about on-premise and cloud architectures.
Advanced Databases This course focuses on the principles, structure, and key features of distributed database systems, covering their design, functionality, and management.
Networks and Distributed Systems This course aims to introduce students to the existence and functioning of communication networks, exploring their past uses and future potential with a focus on distributed systems.
Projects II The course emphasizes teamwork, risk management, effective communication in English, and creating high-quality technical documentation. It also encourages students to reflect on the societal impact of their projects, contributing to the common good.
Statistical Learning and Data Mining This course focuses on teaching techniques and methodologies for extracting relevant information from various data types to design solutions that support decision-making and enable descriptive and predictive tasks.
Artificial Intelligence II This course focuses on sub symbolic intelligent systems, exploring their role in modeling cognitive processes and their technological applications in Deep Learning. It covers key Artificial Intelligence techniques, including connectionist models like neural networks (supervised and unsupervised) for classification and prediction problems, and biologically inspired models like genetic algorithms for optimization.
Professional Ethics and Deontology This course aims to explore ethics as a discipline focused on human fulfillment and happiness through freedom, love, and responsibility. It equips students with fundamental ethical principles and values to guide personal, social, and professional actions, promoting moral discernment and the pursuit of the common good.
4rd year's Subject General Description Learn more
Quality Management This subject will enable students to identify and use metrics to produce high-quality products, execute projects that meet required standards, and adapt to advanced management systems.
High Performance Computing This course focuses on studying current technical solutions, such as parallel, concurrent, and real-time programming, to address computationally complex societal challenges. It covers understanding societal problems requiring supercomputing, familiarizing with widely used supercomputer topologies, designing energy-efficient algorithms to tackle the sustainability challenges of supercomputers, and exploring supercomputing models like MPI and GPGPU.
The God Question in the Digital Era This course explores the meaning of human existence by analyzing the concept of Mystery, examining common answers through reason and faith, and studying the phenomenology of religious experience. It also clarifies the perception of sacred time and space within the secular world and investigates the historical aspects of Christianity and the Church as responses to contemporary human needs.
Security This course focuses on understanding risk analysis and management methodologies as key aspects of Information Systems Security, emphasizing the analysis of organizational business processes, supporting information assets, and potential threats and vulnerabilities.
Knowledge Engineering This course focuses on studying and applying symbolic reasoning techniques in Artificial Intelligence (AI) to solve domain-specific problems using general problem-solving methods. It emphasizes two core elements: problem representation and solution acquisition.
Final Project The Final Degree Project provides students with the opportunity to complete a comprehensive computer project, integrating knowledge from their Computer Engineering studies while adopting an engineering and research-oriented perspective.
Planning and Managing IT Projects This course emphasizes the transition from viewing Computer Systems Management as an art to treating it as an engineering discipline, focusing on effective planning and project management. It highlights the importance of team management and human relationships in achieving successful project outcomes, while aligning projects with client needs and fostering personal and professional growth.
