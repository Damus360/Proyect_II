Informatic Engineering

Informatics Engineering is a field that focuses on the design, development, and implementation of computer systems and software. It combines theoretical knowledge with practical skills, covering areas like:

Subject General Description Learn more
Fundamentals of Computer Engineering This subject aims to provide a global vision of Computer Engineering in both academic and professional points of view. The course gives the students a grasp of the fundamentals on which this engineering is based (mathematics, physics, anthropology and ethics), the future professional careers and the role of a computer engineer in today's society.
Mathematics for Engineering The subject of Mathematics for Engineering I applies and develops knowledge of linear algebra and optimization in real functions of a variable, offering examples of practical application of the different concepts covered. It wants to be an eminently practical subject that guides the student in solving complex problems, not only in solving exercises, and that adapts as much as possible to the reality of the jobs that will be encountered in professional practice.
Logic and Critical Thinking The subject provides the necessary foundation and key skills for the subsequent development of the capacity for action in the field of Big Data together with technological knowledge.
Introduction to Programming The subject focuses on problem solving, building programs that solve simple problems. To do this, it starts from the design of the algorithm, using modular structures and different programming methods, and reaches both its implementation in a structured programming language, using fundamental coding control structures and basic data types, and its subsequent compilation, debugging and documentation.
Knowledge and Skills Management of the Person It is a peculiar subject, which exists to help you in your personal growth during the university stage.
Discrete Mathematics The subject deals with various areas of mathematics such as set theory, relations, finite and modular arithmetic and graphs, among others, of interest as foundations in the training of the future engineer in relation to the processing and storage of information. in computers, to the design and development of algorithms, to the organization inherent to databases based on relationships between sets and to the basis of data structures and operating systems.
Introduction to Cybersecurity The objective of this subject is to teach the basic concepts around Cybersecurity in both the business and consumer world.
Operating Systems Administration The subject Administration of Operating Systems I introduces the student to the role of the operating system within a computer by studying its main functions, presenting the basic file management techniques and training the student for the installation, basic configuration and management of the operating systems.
